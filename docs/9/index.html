Running from a power bank

It's great to use a power bank to power the game, but something to watch out for is that power banks often switch off if there is not much power draw. If your game has times where nothing is happening for several minutes (e.g. between games), and the controls stop responding, this is probably why.

Sometimes this is desirable but other times you don't want this. That is why you should make an "attract mode", or an animation that plays while nothing else is going on. Lighting a few LEDs uses enough power that the power bank will not then switch itself off. You can also buy gadgets that trick power banks, or come up with your own LiPo battery solution.

Adding Sound!

The Plasma 2350W does not have any way to output sound, but it is relatively easy to add a Piezo buzzer for simple bloops and buzzes.

Key Design Challenges

Please come up with your own games. A few things to think about:

  • How do players know what to do? Do you have to explain it or can they figure it out by themselves quickly?
  • How do players know when they are done? Is there a game over screen? Or a "you win" screen? The red flashes are quite effective at this. What do other colours signify?
  • If you have an attract mode, how do you invite players to press the button, and how do they know when the game has started?
  • What about the physical layout of the game - if some of the fairy lights are not visible can it be played?
  • Can you wear your game wrapped around your body? Can people still play?
  • If it is dark, can we use reflections and diffusing material (like tent fabric) to make different kinds of games?
